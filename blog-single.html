Poster Pollution in Dhaka

  • Fatin Ehsan
  • Dhaka City


Dhaka is adorned with posters on every corner. These posters serve various purposes, but their ubiquity poses a double challenge. It not only spoils the city's aesthetic appeal, but it also contributes to environmental pollution.

" In the sea of posters, the truly important ones are often lost or hidden from view. This problem is particularly acute during election periods when the number of posters increases dramatically. It is critical to take immediate steps to address this “poster pollution” in order to protect the beauty and essence of our city. Failure to address this issue could have irreversible consequences for our beloved metropolis."

Poster pollution has a number of serious consequences:

Safety hazards: The excessive presence of posters can obstruct visibility and pose safety hazards, especially in crowded areas.

Property damage: Posters are susceptible to damage from natural elements such as wind and rain, which can harm buildings and infrastructure.

Waste disposal challenges: Disposing of posters in an environmentally responsible manner is a difficult task. Improper disposal can result in them ending up in landfills or incinerators, releasing harmful pollutants into the environment.

We should come forward to solve this problem by following these measurements:

Stringent regulatory measures: The government should enact and strictly enforce laws and regulations governing the placement of posters. These regulations should define specific locations and methods for poster placement and impose fines for non-compliance.

Public awareness campaigns: Public awareness campaigns can play a vital role in educating citizens about the dangers of poster pollution. These campaigns should explain the negative consequences of poster pollution and encourage people to take action to reduce it.

Exploring alternative advertising avenues: It is critical to encourage businesses and organizations to explore alternative, eco-friendly advertising methods. Digital advertising and word-of-mouth marketing are examples of alternatives that have less negative environmental impacts.

By adopting these measures, we can collectively protect our cities from the negative consequences of poster pollution. Poster pollution is a serious issue that requires coordinated efforts from all sectors to preserve the beauty and sustainability of our urban landscapes. I agree that poster pollution is a pressing issue in Dhaka, and the solutions proposed above are just a few steps in the right direction. I am confident that we can all work together to improve the beauty and sustainability of our cities.
